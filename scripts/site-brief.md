## The Markets

### Primary Market
**Grand Rapids, MN** — Itasca County seat. Population ~11,000. Median home price ~$215,000. Hub of northern Minnesota lake country. Mississippi River headwaters. Access to Chippewa National Forest. Mix of in-town residential, rural acreage, and lakefront properties. Strong remote worker and Twin Cities relocator demand. Malcolm Wallaker's primary market.

### Core Markets

**Cohasset, MN** — Small city adjacent to Grand Rapids. Itasca County. Family-oriented, tight-knit. Many residents commute to Grand Rapids. More affordable than Grand Rapids proper.

**Bigfork, MN** — Small town in northern Itasca County. Known for outdoor access, the Big Fork River, and a genuine small-town character. Popular with cabin buyers.

**Deer River, MN** — Itasca County. Small town with strong local identity. Lake country access. More rural character than Grand Rapids.

**Coleraine, MN** — Iron Range community in western Itasca County. Former mining town with affordable housing stock and solid community infrastructure.

**Bovey, MN** — Small Iron Range community adjacent to Coleraine. Very affordable. Quiet, residential character.

**Hibbing, MN** — St. Louis County. Population ~15,000. Largest Iron Range city. Median home price ~$135,000. Birthplace of Bob Dylan and Greyhound Bus. Hull-Rust-Mahoning open pit mine. Hibbing Taconite plant provides economic stability. Among the most affordable housing markets in Minnesota. Bridger Hopkins' primary Iron Range market.

**Keewatin, MN** — Small Iron Range community in Itasca County. Adjacent to Hibbing. Affordable, residential, overlooked by buyers who don't know the area.

**Virginia, MN** — St. Louis County. Population ~8,600. Median home price ~$125,000. Iron Range city surrounded by lakes and forests. Affordable and stable.

**Chisholm, MN** — St. Louis County. Iron Range city. Home to the Minnesota Discovery Center. Affordable housing, strong community character.

**Duluth, MN** — St. Louis County. Population ~90,000. Minnesota's third-largest city. On Lake Superior. Significant price premium over Iron Range markets. Attracts outdoor enthusiasts, remote workers, and retirees. More competitive market.

**Aitkin, MN** — Aitkin County seat. Population ~2,000. On the Mississippi River. Popular with cabin and lake home buyers. Gateway to the Mille Lacs Lake area.

**McGregor, MN** — Aitkin County. Small town. Rural and lake-focused. Quiet market with strong seasonal demand.

## Northern Minnesota Real Estate — What Makes It Unique

- **Property diversity:** From $80K Iron Range starter homes to $1M+ Itasca County lakefront estates. No other region in Minnesota offers this range.
- **Lakefront and cabin market:** Itasca County has hundreds of lakes. Lakefront properties require specific knowledge of DNR shoreland regulations, dock permitting, riparian rights, seasonal vs. year-round distinctions, and lake quality differences.
- **Remote worker demand:** Northern Minnesota has seen meaningful in-migration from Minneapolis/St. Paul as remote workers realize they can own significantly more for the same money.
- **Iron Range stability:** The taconite and mining economy provides employment stability that keeps Iron Range markets from the volatility seen elsewhere.
- **Seasonal dynamics:** Spring and summer bring the most activity. Winter listings can move slower but attract motivated buyers. Seasonal properties require financing knowledge most agents don't have.
- **Local knowledge premium:** An out-of-area agent can't tell you which lakes have invasive species issues, which neighborhoods flood, which roads are seasonal, or where the value is hiding. Local knowledge here is not a marketing claim — it's a functional necessity.
