The Iron Range is undervalued, and serious buyers know it. Hibbing, Virginia, and Chisholm offer some of the lowest price-to-rent ratios in Minnesota, supported by a stable economy anchored by Hibbing Taconite, Minorca Mine, United Taconite, and a healthcare sector centered around Essentia Health and Fairview Range. ## What Types of Investment Properties Perform Best in Hibbing?

Not every cheap house on the Iron Range is a deal. Bridger Hopkins is direct about which property types actually perform as rentals:

**Single-family homes in the $100,000 to $160,000 range** in established Hibbing neighborhoods. These attract long-term tenants, often mine workers or healthcare employees, and produce steady cash flow with manageable turnover.

**Small multifamily properties (duplexes and triplexes)**, when available. Inventory is limited but these are the highest-yielding assets on the Iron Range when priced right.

**Move-in ready homes near downtown Hibbing, Chisholm, or Virginia**, which rent quickly and command premium rents relative to purchase price.

Bridger also tells investors what to avoid. Older homes with deferred maintenance, fuel oil heat that needs converting, knob-and-tube wiring, or settled foundations can wipe out three years of cash flow with one repair. Properties on the wrong block, even at attractive prices, often sit vacant or attract problem tenants. Honest assessment of these factors is what separates a productive Iron Range portfolio from a money pit.
