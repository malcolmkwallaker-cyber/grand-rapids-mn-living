## What Are Home Prices Like in McGregor, MN?

McGregor is one of the more affordable markets in northern Minnesota. In-town residential homes typically range from around $100,000 to $200,000 depending on condition, lot size, and age. Rural properties with acreage run higher based on land, outbuildings, and road access. Lakefront and lake access properties on Sandy Lake and surrounding waters can range from roughly $250,000 for modest seasonal cabins to well over $600,000 for year-round homes with quality frontage.

Seasonal demand drives the McGregor market. Spring and early summer see the most cabin and lake activity. In-town homes move more evenly throughout the year, but winter listings often attract the most motivated buyers. Bridger Hopkins advises sellers on timing based on property type, not on generic market talking points.

## What Should Buyers Know About McGregor and Aitkin County?

McGregor is a small town of roughly 400 residents in northern Aitkin County, about 40 minutes southeast of Grand Rapids and about two and a half hours north of the Twin Cities. It sits near the Savanna State Forest and offers access to Big Sandy Lake, one of the most popular recreational lakes in the region. The economy is rural, driven by tourism, seasonal residents, small business, and commuters working in Aitkin, Grand Rapids, or the Cloquet area.

Buyers moving to McGregor should understand what rural Aitkin County actually looks like. Many properties are on private wells and septic systems. Some roads are seasonal and not maintained year-round. Cell service varies. Broadband is improving but not universal. These are not deal-breakers, but they are real considerations, and Bridger is straightforward with buyers about them before an offer gets written rather than after closing.
