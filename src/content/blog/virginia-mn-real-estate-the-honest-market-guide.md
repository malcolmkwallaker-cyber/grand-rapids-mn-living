## What Home Prices Look Like in Virginia, MN Right Now

The median home price in Virginia sits around $125,000. The Minnesota state median is more than three times that. That's not a typo, and it's not a distressed market. It's just how Iron Range pricing has worked for a long time.

Entry level houses in Virginia, think 2 bed, 1 bath, solid bones, mid century construction, run $80,000 to $115,000. Move up homes with updated kitchens, 3 bedrooms, larger lots, and a garage land in the $140,000 to $190,000 range. Anything above $225,000 in Virginia is going to be a substantially upgraded property or sitting on lake access.

The other thing worth knowing: Virginia Minnesota homes for sale tend to sit a little longer than Grand Rapids listings. That works in a buyer's favor. There's room to negotiate, room to inspect properly, and room to walk if the deal isn't right.

## Virginia's Neighborhoods: What Buyers Should Know

Virginia isn't huge, around 8,600 people, but the neighborhoods do have their own character. The area north of Chestnut Street, closer to Silver Lake, holds some of the more established homes with mature trees and bigger lots. Prices climb a bit here, but not dramatically.

The south side, closer to Highway 53 and the commercial corridor, is where you'll find more affordable starter homes and rental investment opportunities. The housing stock is older, often 1940s to 1960s builds, and condition varies a lot house to house. This is where a local inspection matters.

West Virginia, the section closer to Mountain Iron, blurs the line between the two cities. A lot of buyers shop both Virginia and Mountain Iron at the same time because the price points overlap and the school options are right there. If you're looking at virginia mn real estate, you should be looking at Mountain Iron listings too.

Here's the value argument in plain numbers. A buyer relocating from the Twin Cities metro who sells a $450,000 starter home in Maple Grove can buy a fully updated home in Virginia for under $200,000, pay cash, and still have $250,000 left over. Virginia is surrounded by lakes. Silver Lake is right in town. Bass Lake, Sand Lake, Esquagama Lake, Lake Vermilion, all within a 20 to 35 minute drive. Lake access changes the math entirely when you compare what an equivalent lake area home costs in central Minnesota or Wisconsin.

Iron range real estate Virginia listings also include something most buyers don't expect: actual land. Half acre and three quarter acre in town lots are normal here, not premium. In the Twin Cities that lot alone would cost more than the entire house does in Virginia.

## The Iron Range Economy and What It Means for Stability

Minntac, the U.S. Steel taconite operation in nearby Mountain Iron, is the largest taconite plant in the world. It employs around 1,400 people directly and supports thousands more in the regional economy. That plant has been operating since 1967.

What that means for the Virginia housing market is stability. Iron Range home values don't spike the way Twin Cities suburbs do, but they also don't collapse. Through 2008, through 2020, through every recent cycle, this market has held a steady floor. The numbers here are hard to argue with.

Healthcare is the other employment anchor. Essentia Health Virginia is a major regional hospital and a significant employer. Between mining, healthcare, education, and the trades, the local job base is more diversified than people assume.

The Iron Range is undervalued, and serious buyers know it. The buyers who don't know it are the ones still paying $400,000 for a townhouse in Woodbury.

## What Out of State Buyers Are Consistently Surprised By

A few things catch out of area buyers off guard when they actually visit. First, the lot sizes. Second, how close real lake access is to town. Third, the quality of the housing stock at this price point. A lot of these homes were built by mining families who took care of them.

Fourth, the property taxes. They're reasonable. A $150,000 home in Virginia typically runs $1,500 to $2,200 in annual property tax, depending on the specific parcel. That's a fraction of what comparable taxes look like downstate.

Fifth, and this one matters: not every property in Virginia is on city sewer and water. Some homes on the edges of town are on well and septic. That's not a problem, but it changes your inspection list and your financing options. This is where a local agent saves you from a bad surprise.
